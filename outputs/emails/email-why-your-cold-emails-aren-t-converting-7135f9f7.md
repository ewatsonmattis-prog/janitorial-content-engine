Hi [First Name],

Most cleaning companies chasing office contracts have the same problem — and it's not a lack of leads.

They send a cold email. Maybe two. They get no reply. They move on. And six months later, that same prospect signs with a competitor who followed up five times.

The issue isn't your targeting. It's the system behind the outreach.

Here's what actually wins office cleaning contracts through cold email:

First, your opening email needs to do one job — earn a reply, not close a sale. Most cold emails try to explain every service, every accreditation, and every client you've ever had. Cut it down. One relevant observation, one clear reason you're reaching out, one simple question.

Second, the follow-up sequence is where contracts are actually won. Research consistently shows that most positive responses to cold outreach come after the third, fourth, or fifth touchpoint. If you're stopping at one or two, you're doing the hard work and handing the reward to whoever follows up next.

Third, trust is built through repetition, not persuasion. Every follow-up email is a chance to add something useful — a relevant case study, a specific insight about their building type, a question about their current contract renewal date. You're not chasing. You're staying visible until the timing is right.

Fourth, the goal of the entire sequence is one outcome: a booked conversation. Not a PDF download. Not a website visit. A real sales conversation with a decision-maker who has a cleaning contract to place.

This is the difference between scattered outreach and a cold email system that actually fills your pipeline with qualified office cleaning opportunities.

If your current outreach isn't generating consistent sales conversations, the problem is almost certainly in the structure — not the effort you're putting in.
