## Post 2

7 reasons your cold emails to facilities managers aren't converting — and what to fix first.

Most cleaning companies send emails. Very few win contracts from them.

Here's what separates the ones that do:

1. They lead with a specific problem, not a company intro.
   FMs don't care who you are on email one. They care whether you understand their site.

2. They mention building type or sector in the subject line.
   "Cleaning for multi-tenant office blocks in [City]" outperforms "Commercial Cleaning Services" every time.

3. They reference cleaning frequency realistically.
   Generic emails say "regular cleaning services." Specific emails say "5-day-a-week daytime cleans for open-plan offices."

4. They follow up — twice.
   Most replies come on email two or three. Sending one email and stopping is leaving contracts on the table.

5. They have one clear ask.
   Not "let us know if you're interested." Try: "Would a 10-minute call this week make sense?"

6. They don't attach a brochure to email one.
   Attachments trigger spam filters and kill open rates. Save it for the reply.

7. They track what's working.
   Open rates, reply rates, which sectors respond — without this data, you're guessing.

How to write a 3-email cold sequence that gets facilities managers to reply — step by step.

Most cleaning companies send one email and wait.

Facilities managers are busy. Their inbox is full. One email isn't enough.

Here's how to build a 3-email sequence that converts at 3–5x the rate of a generic pitch:

---

EMAIL 1 — The Specific Problem Email
Send day 1.

Subject: Cleaning for [sector] sites in [City]

Open with a problem specific to their industry — not a company intro.

Example for office FM:
"Managing cleaning across occupied open-plan offices usually means one of two things — either you're running evening cleans that no one checks, or you're handling tenant complaints about standards."

Then: one sentence on who you are and what you do.
Close with: "Worth a quick call this week to see if we'd be a fit?"

---

EMAIL 2 — The Follow-Up With Evidence
Send day 4–5.

Don't apologise for following up.

Add one proof point — a relevant site type you service, a contract retained for 3+ years, a sector-specific accreditation.

Keep it to 4 lines maximum.

---

EMAIL 3 — The Short Close
Send day 9–10.

3 lines only:
— Acknowledge it might not be the right time
— Leave the door open
— Make it easy to reply with one word

Example: "If you're locked into a contract for now, happy to reconnect closer to renewal. Worth me keeping in touch?"

---
