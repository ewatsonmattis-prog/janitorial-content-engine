**Preview Text:** It's not the list. It's what happens after the first email.

Hi [First Name],

Most cleaning companies sending cold emails are doing the same thing: a one-shot message, a generic pitch, and then silence.

That's not lead generation. That's hoping.

Here's what's actually happening on the other side of your emails. The facilities manager or office manager you're targeting receives your message, doesn't have an immediate need, and moves on. Not because they don't care about cleaning. Because the timing wasn't right and nothing pulled them back.

The companies winning commercial contracts consistently aren't sending better first emails. They're building a system that stays in front of prospects until the timing is right.

That means a sequence — not a single email. It means following up three, four, five times across several weeks with messages that add value, not just chase a reply. It means referencing their sector, their building type, their likely pain points. A school has different cleaning pressures to a logistics warehouse. Your outreach should reflect that.

It also means being ready when they do respond. A lead that replies to email four in your sequence is warm. If your follow-up takes 48 hours, or worse, lands in a generic inbox no one monitors, that opportunity is gone.

The businesses we work with don't chase random leads. They build a prospecting engine: targeted lists of the right decision-makers, sequenced outreach that builds familiarity and trust, and a clear path from first reply to booked conversation.

This isn't about volume. Blasting five hundred emails a week and hoping for a 0.5% reply rate is not a growth strategy. Targeting fifty well-researched prospects with a thoughtful, consistent sequence will outperform that every time.

If you're already sending cold emails but not booking enough conversations, the problem is almost certainly not the first message. It's everything that comes after it.
