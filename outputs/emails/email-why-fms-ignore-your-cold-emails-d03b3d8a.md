Hi [First Name],

Most cold emails sent by cleaning companies get deleted in under three seconds.

Not because the service is bad. Not because the timing is wrong. Because the email reads like it was written for no one in particular — and facilities managers can tell instantly.

Here's what's actually happening on their end.

FMs responsible for office buildings, schools, and healthcare sites receive a lot of contractor outreach. They're not short of options. What they're short of is time. So when an email lands that says something like "we provide professional cleaning services to businesses in your area" — it goes straight to trash. It signals that you haven't done five minutes of research.

What gets a reply is specificity.

A 3-email sequence that references their sector (say, multi-tenanted offices or NHS-affiliated clinics), acknowledges the likely cleaning frequency their building requires, and speaks directly to the compliance or footfall pressures they face — that sequence converts at 3 to 5 times the rate of a generic pitch.

The first email earns attention by showing you understand their environment. The second adds a relevant proof point — a similar building you service, a problem you solved. The third is a short, low-pressure follow-up that makes it easy to say yes to a conversation.

This isn't about clever copywriting. It's about doing the minimum research that most of your competitors won't bother with.

Facilities managers shortlist contractors who feel like they already understand the job before they've even quoted. Responsiveness matters too — if you follow up within 48 hours and your message is relevant, you're already ahead of 80% of the field.
