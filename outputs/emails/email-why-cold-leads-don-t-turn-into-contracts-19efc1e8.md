**Preview Text:** Getting replies isn't the hard part. Converting them into signed contracts is.

Hi [First Name],

Most cleaning companies chasing office contracts have the same problem — and it's not a lack of leads.

They get a few replies. Someone asks for a quote. Then nothing. The prospect goes cold and the opportunity disappears.

Here's what's actually happening.

Office managers and facilities buyers don't switch cleaning contractors on impulse. They have existing suppliers, inertia works against you, and they're not going to hand over a recurring contract to a company they've heard from once. Trust takes repetition. One cold email isn't a system — it's a shot in the dark.

The cleaning companies consistently winning office contracts aren't sending better emails. They're following up more deliberately. A sequence that starts with a specific, relevant outreach message — not a generic "we clean offices" pitch — and then follows up two, three, four times over several weeks. Each touchpoint adds a small amount of credibility. A case study. A relevant question. A short proof point.

By the time the prospect replies, they already feel like they know you.

There's a second piece most companies miss: timing. Office cleaning contracts often become available when something goes wrong — a current supplier lets them down, a lease renewal triggers a procurement review, headcount changes. Your job is to be in front of the right prospects consistently enough that when that moment arrives, you're the name they remember.

That only happens if you have a pipeline that runs every week — not a burst of outreach every time you need new business.

If your current approach to winning office contracts looks like occasional outreach, sporadic follow-up, and waiting for referrals, you don't have a lead generation system. You have a hope strategy.

Building a predictable pipeline for commercial contracts isn't complicated. But it does require the right sequences, the right targeting, and consistent execution — week in, week out.
