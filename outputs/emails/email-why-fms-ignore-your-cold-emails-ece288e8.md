Hi [First Name],

Most cold emails sent by cleaning companies get deleted in under three seconds. Not because facilities managers are too busy — but because the email reads like it was written for no one in particular.

Facilities managers are responsible for buildings. Specific buildings. With specific footfall, specific compliance requirements, and specific cleaning schedules. When your email doesn't reflect that, it signals you haven't done the work — and they move on.

Here's what actually converts: a 3-email sequence built around what they care about.

Email one references their industry. Healthcare FMs think about infection control. Education FMs think about turnaround between terms. Office FMs think about out-of-hours access and presentation for clients. Lead with the problem they're already trying to solve.

Email two gets specific about their building. If you can reference approximate square footage, number of floors, or building type, do it. It shows you've looked. Even a line like "For a building your size, most contractors we work with schedule deep cleans fortnightly" demonstrates you understand their world.

Email three is a simple follow-up. Short. Direct. No apology for following up. Just one line confirming you're still available and one low-friction ask — a 15-minute call, not a full proposal.

This sequence converts at 3 to 5 times the rate of a generic pitch. Not because it's clever. Because it's relevant.

The facilities managers who respond aren't doing you a favour. They're responding because you've made it easy to see you're worth their time.
