# Email Newsletter: Why cold emails aren't winning you contracts

**Subject Line:** Why cold emails aren't winning you contracts
**Preview Text:** It's not the list. It's what happens after the first email.
**Word Count:** 342

---

Hi [First Name],

Most cleaning company owners who try cold email give up after two weeks.

They send a few hundred emails, get a handful of opens, maybe one or two replies that go nowhere — and conclude that cold email doesn't work.

It does work. The problem isn't the channel. It's the system behind it.

Here's what's actually happening. A facilities manager receives your email on a Tuesday when they're already mid-contract with another cleaning company. They're not ready to switch. But in four months, that contract is up for renewal and they'll be looking. If you sent one email and moved on, you don't exist when that moment arrives.

The cleaning companies winning recurring office contracts aren't the ones with the best one-liner. They're the ones still in the conversation ninety days later.

That requires a follow-up sequence — not aggressive, not daily, but consistent. A second email that adds value. A third that references something specific to their business. A fourth that makes it easy to book a quick call. Most owners send one email and wait. That's not a system. That's a lottery.

Trust is built through repetition and relevance. A facilities manager who sees your name three or four times, each time with something useful or credible, starts to associate you with reliability before you've even spoken. That association is what gets you the meeting.

The other piece most cleaning businesses miss is specificity. Generic emails about "professional cleaning services" land like noise. Emails that reference the prospect's industry, building type, or a known problem — contractor reliability, audit compliance, staff turnover — land differently. They signal that you understand the environment you'd be working in.

You don't need a huge list. You need a targeted list, a structured sequence, and a follow-up process that runs without you chasing it manually every day.

If your current outreach isn't producing booked sales conversations consistently, the gap is almost always in the system — not the effort you're putting in.
