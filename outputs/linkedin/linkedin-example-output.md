## Post 1 — Observation

Most cleaning companies wonder why their cold emails get no replies.

The answer is usually one line: the first sentence is about them, not the prospect.

"Hi, we're XYZ Cleaning, based in Manchester..." — no facilities manager cares. They delete it before they reach the second sentence.

What works is opening on the prospect's problem. Something like: "Managing cleaning across multiple sites is harder than it looks when contractors don't communicate."

That line stops the scroll because it describes exactly what the reader is dealing with.

The rest of the email doesn't need to be clever. One pain. One proof. One ask.

That's the formula that gets replies from busy facilities managers.

Every cleaning company I speak to says cold email doesn't work. Usually it does — they just haven't changed the first line.

## Post 2 — List

5 cold email mistakes cleaning companies keep making (and how to fix them):

1. **Subject line: "Commercial Cleaning Services"**
Nobody opens that. Try: "Quick question about your cleaning setup, [First Name]"

2. **Starting with "I hope this email finds you well"**
This signals you have nothing useful to say. Open with their problem instead.

3. **Sending to generic addresses (info@, enquiries@)**
Facilities managers have names. Find them on LinkedIn. Email the decision-maker.

4. **Writing 300-word emails**
Cut it to 80 words. One problem. One solution. One ask. Busy people reply to short emails.

5. **Asking for "30 minutes of your time"**
That's a big ask from a stranger. Ask if they'd be open to a quick conversation first.

Fix these five things before you write another email.

## Post 5 — How-To

How to write a cold email that facilities managers actually reply to:

**Step 1: Find the right person**
Not info@. Not enquiries@. The facilities manager or office manager by name. LinkedIn + company website usually gets you there.

**Step 2: Write a subject line that sounds human**
"Cleaning at [Company] offices" or "[City] office cleaning — quick question"
Not: "Commercial Cleaning Services for [Company]"

**Step 3: Open on their problem, not your company**
"Managing cleaning contractors across multiple sites usually means chasing someone every week."
Not: "Hi, I'm [Name] from XYZ Cleaning..."

**Step 4: One proof point**
One sentence. One result. Nothing vague.
"We manage cleaning for 12 office buildings in [City] with 100% compliance on audits."

**Step 5: One low-friction ask**
"Would it be worth a quick chat to see if we could take one thing off your plate?"
Not: "Are you free for a 30-minute call this week?"

**That's the whole email. Under 80 words.**

Try it on 20 prospects before you decide it doesn't work.
