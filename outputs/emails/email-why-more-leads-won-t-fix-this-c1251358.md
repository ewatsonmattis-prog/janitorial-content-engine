Most cleaning company owners who come to us aren't struggling because they can't find leads. They're struggling because every lead they get goes cold before it turns into a contract.

The usual response is to go and find more leads. Post more on LinkedIn. Buy a list. Try another directory. It feels like action, but it doesn't fix the actual problem.

The problem is the system — or the lack of one.

When a facilities manager receives a cold email from a cleaning company, they rarely respond immediately. Not because they're not interested, but because it landed at the wrong moment. They saved it to come back to. They got pulled into a meeting. Life happened.

If your only follow-up is one reminder email two weeks later, that contract went to whoever followed up better — not whoever cleaned better.

What actually converts cold outreach into booked conversations is a structured sequence. A first email that speaks directly to their pain — not a generic intro about how long you've been established. A follow-up that adds something useful. A third touch that makes it easy to take the next step. And a system that tracks all of it so nothing falls through the gaps.

This isn't about sending more emails. It's about building a repeatable process that keeps you front of mind until the prospect is ready to act.

The cleaning companies winning recurring office contracts right now aren't necessarily the biggest or the cheapest. They're the ones who show up consistently, communicate with confidence, and have a sales process that doesn't rely on someone remembering to chase.

If your pipeline feels unpredictable — feast one month, nothing the next — it's usually a signal that you have lead generation activity, but not a lead generation system.
