Hi [First Name],

Most cleaning companies aren't losing contracts because of their service. They're losing them because their follow-up falls apart after the first email.

Here's what usually happens. A facilities manager opens your cold email, thinks it looks relevant, and does nothing. Not because they're not interested — because they're busy and you didn't make it easy enough to take the next step.

So the lead goes cold. You move on. They eventually sign with someone else who followed up three more times.

The problem isn't lead volume. The problem is that most cleaning businesses treat cold outreach as a one-shot process. One email, one call, maybe one more email if you remember. That's not a system — that's hoping.

A real cold outreach system does three things well. First, it identifies the right prospects — facilities managers, property managers, and operations leads at businesses where you can actually deliver recurring value. Second, it sends a clear, credible opening message that speaks directly to their situation, not a generic pitch. Third — and this is where most cleaning companies fail — it follows up consistently, across multiple touchpoints, over several weeks.

Trust is built in the follow-up, not the first message.

Facilities managers are not waiting for your email. They have a current provider. They're not actively shopping. But contracts do come up for renewal. Decisions do get triggered by poor service, staff changes, or new site requirements. If you've been showing up consistently in their inbox with relevant, non-pushy messages, you're the first call they make.

That's the position you want to be in — and it doesn't happen by accident.
