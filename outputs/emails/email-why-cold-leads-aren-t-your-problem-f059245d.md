Hi [First Name],

Most commercial cleaning companies don't have a leads problem. They have a system problem.

You've probably sent cold emails before. Maybe you've tried a lead list, a LinkedIn message, or a local directory. You got a few replies. Nothing converted. You moved on.

The issue isn't that cold outreach doesn't work for cleaning contracts. The issue is that a one-shot email with no follow-up and no structure isn't outreach — it's a lottery ticket.

Facilities managers and office managers are busy. They're not waiting for your email. Even if the timing is right and they're genuinely looking for a new cleaning contractor, they'll forget about you within 48 hours if you don't follow up.

Here's what a working cold email system actually looks like: a short, specific email that speaks to their situation — not a generic "we offer competitive rates" pitch. Followed by three to five touchpoints over two to three weeks. Each one adding a small piece of credibility or value. A case study. A relevant question. A simple re-open.

That sequence turns a 2% reply rate into a 12–15% conversation rate. Not because you're a better writer. Because you stayed in the frame long enough for timing to work in your favour.

The other piece most cleaning companies miss is targeting. Sending 500 emails to a mixed list of random businesses is noise. Sending 80 emails to facilities managers at multi-site office buildings in your region, with a sequence built around their specific pain points — that's a pipeline.

You don't need more leads. You need a repeatable system that builds familiarity, earns trust, and gets the right people onto a call with you.
