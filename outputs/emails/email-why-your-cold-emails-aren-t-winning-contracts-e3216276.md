Hi [First Name],

Most cleaning company owners we speak to have tried cold email. Some paid for a list. Some sent a few hundred emails. A handful got replies. Almost none turned those replies into signed contracts.

The problem isn't the email. It's what happens after.

Cold outreach for commercial cleaning contracts doesn't fail at the first touch. It fails because there's no system behind it. One email goes out, silence comes back, and the lead gets forgotten. Meanwhile, a competitor with a slower pitch but a smarter follow-up sequence wins the contract.

Here's the insight most cleaning business owners miss: facilities managers and office managers are not waiting to be sold to. They're busy, they already have a cleaner, and they're not actively looking to switch. Your job isn't to interrupt them with a pitch — it's to stay visible, build familiarity, and be the obvious choice when their current contract frustrates them.

That requires a system, not a one-off send.

A working cold email system for commercial cleaning contracts has three parts. First, a targeted outreach sequence — not one email, but a structured series of four to six touchpoints spread over three to four weeks. Second, a reason to reply that isn't just 'we're a great cleaning company' — it's a specific, relevant observation about their sector, location, or situation. Third, a clear next step that feels low-friction, like a short conversation rather than a full proposal.

When those three things work together, cold email stops being a lottery and becomes a predictable pipeline.
