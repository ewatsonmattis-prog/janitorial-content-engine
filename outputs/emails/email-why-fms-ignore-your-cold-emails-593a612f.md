Hi [First Name],

Most cold emails cleaning companies send get deleted in under three seconds.

Not because the service is bad. Because the email looks like it was written for anyone — which means it feels relevant to no one.

Facilities managers receive outreach constantly. What makes them stop and read? When something in the first two lines reflects their world. Their building type. Their sector. A problem specific to their setup.

Generic pitch: "We provide professional commercial cleaning services to businesses across the region."

Specific pitch: "We work with mid-size office buildings in the 10,000–30,000 sq ft range — typically doing 3x weekly cleans. If your current contractor is inconsistent on early-morning attendance, that's exactly the gap we help close."

One of those gets a reply. The other gets archived.

The approach that consistently works is a 3-email sequence built around three things: the prospect's industry, their likely building size, and their cleaning frequency. That level of specificity signals that you've done five minutes of research — and that alone separates you from 95% of the outreach they receive.

Sequence structure that works:

Email 1 — Short intro referencing their sector and a common pain point (e.g., compliance requirements in healthcare, footfall pressure in education).

Email 2 — Sent 4 days later. One sentence acknowledging they're busy, followed by a single relevant proof point (a client result, a retained contract length, a specific location).

Email 3 — Sent 5 days after that. A low-friction close: "Not the right time?" or "Happy to send over what we'd typically propose for a building your size?"

This sequence converts at 3–5x the rate of a single generic email. Not because it's clever — because it's relevant.
