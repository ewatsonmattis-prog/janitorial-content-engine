## Post 2

6 reasons your cold emails to facilities managers aren't converting (and what to fix):

1. You opened with "I" instead of them
Every email starting with "I am writing to introduce..." loses before it starts. Open with a reference to their building, sector, or a problem they recognise.

2. You sent one email and gave up
Facilities managers manage dozens of supplier relationships. One email is not a sequence. A 3-touch sequence — spaced 4–6 days apart — dramatically increases reply rates.

3. You didn't mention their industry
A school bursar and an office facilities manager have completely different concerns. Compliance, infection control, term-time schedules, DBS requirements. If your email could apply to anyone, it applies to no one.

4. You listed services instead of outcomes
"We offer daily cleaning, washroom services, and waste removal" tells them nothing useful. "We help education facilities maintain cleaning compliance between inspections" tells them exactly what matters.

5. You asked for too much too soon
Asking for a meeting in the first email is like proposing on a first introduction. Ask for a 10-minute call or whether they have a contract review coming up.

6. You sent it from a Gmail address
A Gmail or Hotmail address signals you're a sole trader operating informally. Set up a branded domain email. It costs under £10/year and instantly increases credibility.

How to write a cold email sequence that gets facilities managers to reply (3-email framework):

Most cleaning companies send one email and call it outreach.

Here's a 3-email sequence that consistently generates replies from facilities managers in offices, healthcare, and education:

---

EMAIL 1 — The Relevant Introduction (Day 1)

Subject: Cleaning cover for [Building Type] in [Location]

Open by referencing something specific to their sector — not your business.

Example for healthcare: "Facilities managers in healthcare settings are increasingly being asked to evidence cleaning schedules for CQC compliance. We work with several NHS-adjacent sites in [region] to make that documentation straightforward."

Then: one sentence on what you do, one sentence on a relevant result, and a soft CTA — asking if they have a contract review scheduled in the next quarter.

---

EMAIL 2 — The Follow-Up With Value (Day 5)

Don't just say "following up on my last email."

Add something useful. A checklist. A question about their current cleaning frequency. A reference to a compliance requirement relevant to their sector.

Show you understand their world without a contract yet.

---

EMAIL 3 — The Honest Close (Day 10)

Keep it short. Acknowledge it may not be the right time. Give them an easy out — and an easy yes.

"If your current contract is working well, no action needed. If a review is coming up and you'd like an alternative quote, a 10-minute call is all it takes."

---
