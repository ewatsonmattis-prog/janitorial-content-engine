# How to Win Facilities Manager Contracts With Cold Email: A Practical Guide for Commercial Cleaning Companies

Most commercial cleaning companies struggle to grow beyond referrals. Word-of-mouth works — until it stops, and there's nothing in the pipeline to replace it.

Cold email is the fastest way to build a predictable contract acquisition system. But most cleaning companies do it wrong, and they conclude it doesn't work. This guide shows you exactly how to do it right: who to target, what to write, and how to turn replies into contracts.

---

## Why Most Cleaning Company Cold Emails Fail

The average cold email from a cleaning company looks like this:

*"Hi, we're ABC Cleaning, a commercial cleaning company based in Manchester. We've been operating for 10 years and offer a full range of commercial cleaning services. Could we arrange a call to discuss your cleaning needs?"*

This email fails for three reasons:

1. **It's about the company, not the prospect.** The facilities manager doesn't care about your history. They care about their problem.
2. **It's too long and too generic.** "Full range of commercial cleaning services" says nothing specific.
3. **The ask is too big.** Busy facilities managers don't schedule calls with strangers on request.

The bar is low. Most cleaning companies send the same generic email to every prospect. To stand out, you don't need to be brilliant — you just need to be specific.

---

## Who to Target: The Right Facilities Manager Segments

Before you write a word, segment your prospect list. A facilities manager at a primary school has completely different concerns from an office manager at a tech company. Writing one email for both means it resonates with neither.

**Productive segments for commercial cleaning outreach:**

- **Office buildings (50–500 staff):** Care about reliability, minimal disruption, and a single point of contact
- **Healthcare facilities:** Prioritise compliance, infection control, and documented cleaning schedules
- **Schools and education:** Need DBS-checked staff, holiday and term-time flexibility, and clear audit trails
- **Warehouses and logistics centres:** Want industrial cleaning, flexible hours, and no disruption to operations
- **Property management companies:** Manage multiple sites — a single contractor who covers all of them is valuable

Pick one segment and write specifically to them. You can expand later.

## The 3-Email Cold Sequence That Gets Replies

A single cold email rarely converts. A short, well-spaced sequence of three emails outperforms one email by 3–4x.

### Email 1: The Problem Open

**Subject:** Cleaning at [Company] offices — quick question

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Managing commercial cleaning across an office building usually means chasing your contractor at some point. Either something gets missed, or communication is inconsistent.

We work with office managers in [City] to take that off their plate — one point of contact, consistent cleaning, and documented quality checks.

Would it be worth a 10-minute call to see if we'd be a fit?

[Your name]

**Why it works:**
- Under 80 words
- Opens on their problem, not your company
- Proof point is specific to their segment
- Ask is low-friction

---

### Email 2: Social Proof (3 days later)

**Subject:** Re: Cleaning at [Company] offices

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Just following up.

We recently took over the cleaning contract for a 200-person office in [City]. Their previous contractor had a 35% complaint rate. In the first three months, that dropped to zero.

If your cleaning setup is working well, no need to change it. But if there are gaps, happy to share how we approached it.

Worth a quick chat?

[Your name]

---

### Email 3: The Close (5 days later)

**Subject:** Last email — [Company]

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Last email from me on this.

If the timing isn't right or you're happy with your current setup, no worries at all. Happy to reconnect when it makes sense.

If you are looking for a more reliable cleaning contractor for [Company], I'd be glad to talk through what we do.

[Your name] | [Phone number]

## Finding the Right Contact

Sending cold emails to generic addresses (info@, enquiries@) is one of the most common mistakes. These inboxes are managed by administrators who filter out supplier approaches.

Find the decision-maker:

1. **LinkedIn** — Search the company name + "facilities manager" or "office manager"
2. **Company website** — Team pages, contact pages, case studies
3. **Apollo.io or Hunter.io** — Email finding tools with accuracy rates of 70–85%
4. **Google** — "[Company name] facilities manager LinkedIn"

For smaller companies (under 50 staff), the office manager or business owner is usually the decision-maker.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Sending too early in the morning or late in the day.** Tuesday to Thursday, between 8–10am or 1–3pm, has the highest open and reply rates for B2B.

**Following up too quickly.** Wait 3–5 business days between emails. Daily follow-up is aggressive and damages your reputation.

**Using read-receipt trackers.** Many email clients block these, and savvy recipients find them off-putting.

**Making it about price.** Never mention pricing in a cold email. You're trying to start a conversation, not close a deal.

**Sending from a shared inbox.** Replies to info@ or sales@ feel less personal. Send from a named individual.

## Tracking What Works

Once your sequence is running, track these four metrics:

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Open rate | 35–50% |
| Reply rate | 3–6% |
| Positive reply rate | 1–2% |
| Reply to meeting rate | 50–60% |

If your open rate is low, the problem is your subject line. If your reply rate is low, the problem is your first email. If your reply-to-meeting rate is low, the problem is how you're handling replies.

## What Happens After the Reply

The biggest opportunity lost in cleaning company outreach is the follow-up after someone replies. When a facilities manager responds positively, most cleaning companies take too long to reply or move too slowly to a meeting.

**Response rule:** Reply within 2 hours during business hours.

**First response structure:**
1. Acknowledge their interest in one sentence
2. Suggest two specific call times
3. Confirm what the call covers (15–20 minutes, no hard sell)

Speed and ease of response signals to the prospect that you're responsive. That's exactly the quality they want from a cleaning contractor.

## Summary

Cold email works for commercial cleaning companies when you:

1. Target one specific segment at a time
2. Write to the prospect's problem, not your company's history
3. Keep emails under 80 words
4. Use a 3-email sequence, not one blast
5. Find named decision-makers, not generic inboxes
6. Follow up promptly when someone replies

It takes a week to set up a sequence and two weeks to see initial results. Most cleaning companies give up after sending one email to a cold list. The ones that stay consistent build a predictable pipeline of commercial contract enquiries.
