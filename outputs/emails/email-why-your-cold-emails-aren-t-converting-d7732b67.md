# Email Newsletter: Why your cold emails aren't converting

**Subject Line:** Why your cold emails aren't converting
**Preview Text:** It's not your list. It's what happens after the first email.
**Word Count:** 342

---

Hi [First Name],

Most cleaning companies chasing office contracts make the same mistake. They send one cold email, get no reply, and assume the prospect isn't interested.

That's not how decisions get made.

Facility managers and office directors are busy. They're not ignoring you because they don't need cleaning. They're ignoring you because you haven't given them enough reason to stop and respond — yet.

Here's the real problem: a single cold email isn't a system. It's a shot in the dark. What actually wins recurring office contracts is a sequence that builds familiarity, addresses objections, and keeps your name visible until the timing is right.

That means following up. Not once. Three, four, sometimes five times — across email and LinkedIn — over three to four weeks. Each touchpoint should add something: a relevant observation about their building type, a result you've achieved for a similar client, a low-friction offer like a free site audit.

The companies consistently winning office contracts aren't necessarily the best cleaners. They're the ones with the most consistent outreach process. They show up repeatedly, professionally, and with a clear reason to respond.

There's also a targeting problem worth fixing. Generic prospect lists waste your time. A focused list of 50 decision-makers in your local commercial market — office managers, property managers, estate agents managing commercial space — will outperform a spray-and-pray list of 500 every time.

And when someone does reply? That's where most owners drop the ball. No follow-up process, no booked call, no proposal. The lead goes cold because there's no system on the back end to convert interest into a signed contract.

Building this kind of pipeline isn't complicated. But it does require consistency, the right messaging, and a process that doesn't depend on you remembering to follow up.

If your current outreach isn't producing regular booked calls with office prospects, it's worth looking at the system — not just the email.
