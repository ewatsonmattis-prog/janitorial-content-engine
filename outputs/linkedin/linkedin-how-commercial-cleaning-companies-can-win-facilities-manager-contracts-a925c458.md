## Post 2

6 reasons your cold emails to facilities managers aren't converting (and how to fix each one):

1. You're leading with your company, not their problem.
FMs don't care who you are yet. Open with something they recognise: "Most FMs managing multi-floor office blocks tell us contractor reliability is their biggest headache."

2. Your subject line is invisible.
"Cleaning Services Quote" is deleted on autopilot. Try: "Cleaning cover for [Building Type] in [City]" — it reads like a conversation, not a pitch.

3. You're sending one email and giving up.
FMs are busy. A 3-email sequence — intro, follow-up, final check-in — converts at 3–5x the rate of a single send.

4. You're not referencing their industry.
Healthcare cleaning has compliance angles. Schools have term-time schedules. Offices have out-of-hours requirements. Show you understand their world.

5. You're asking for too much too soon.
Don't lead with "Can we arrange a site visit?" Lead with "Would a quick 10-minute call make sense?" — lower friction, higher response rate.

6. Your email has no specificity.
Building size. Cleaning frequency. Sector-relevant pain point. Three details that turn a cold email into a relevant one.

Facilities managers shortlist contractors who feel prepared before they've even met them.

How to write a 3-email cold sequence that gets facilities managers to reply (step by step):

Most cleaning companies send one generic email and wonder why no one responds.

Here's the exact structure that converts at 3–5x that rate:

---

📧 EMAIL 1 — The Relevant Intro (Day 1)

Subject: [Sector] cleaning for [Building Type] in [City]

Open with their pain, not your pitch.
Example: "Most facilities managers overseeing office buildings in [City] tell us their biggest frustration is contractors who go quiet after the first month."

Then:
— One line on who you are
— Reference their building type and likely cleaning frequency
— One soft CTA: "Worth a 10-minute call this week?"

Keep it under 120 words.

---

📧 EMAIL 2 — The Follow-Up (Day 4–5)

Don't resend the first email. Add value.

Reference something sector-specific:
"We've recently helped [similar building type] in [City] reduce cleaning gaps during peak hours — happy to share what worked if it's useful."

Same CTA. Keep it short.

---

📧 EMAIL 3 — The Close (Day 10–12)

Be direct. This is your last touch.

"I won't keep following up — but if regular commercial cleaning cover for [building/site] ever becomes a priority, we'd be ready to move quickly."

Leave the door open. No pressure. No apology.

---

Three emails. Plain text. Sector-specific language.
