Hi [First Name],

Most cold emails to facilities managers get deleted in under three seconds.

Not because the cleaning company is bad at the job. Because the email reads like it was sent to 500 people — because it was.

Facilities managers are responsible for keeping buildings running. They're busy, they're accountable, and they've seen every generic "we offer a full range of commercial cleaning services" pitch imaginable. Yours needs to be different from the first line.

Here's what actually moves the needle: specificity.

When you reference their industry — say, multi-floor office buildings or NHS-compliant healthcare facilities — they read on. When you mention a realistic cleaning frequency for a site their size, it signals you understand their operation. When you address a common pain point for their sector (like contractor no-shows before an audit, or inconsistent restroom checks in a high-footfall building), you sound like someone worth talking to.

A 3-email sequence built around these three elements — industry relevance, building context, and a sector-specific pain point — consistently converts at 3 to 5 times the rate of a generic outreach email. The sequence works like this: email one opens the conversation with a specific observation, email two adds a proof point or case study from a similar facility, email three is a short, low-pressure follow-up.

None of the emails should be long. None should lead with your company. All of them should make the FM feel like you've done five minutes of research on their situation — because you have.

This is how cleaning companies with no brand recognition win contracts from facilities managers who've never heard of them. Not by shouting louder. By being more relevant.
