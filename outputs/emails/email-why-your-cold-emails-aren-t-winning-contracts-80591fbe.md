Hi [First Name],

Most commercial cleaning companies that come to us have tried cold email. They built a list, sent a few hundred emails, got a handful of replies, and then... nothing converted.

So they concluded cold email doesn't work. That's the wrong conclusion.

The problem isn't the channel. The problem is that a single cold email isn't a system. It's a one-time shot with no structure behind it — and facilities managers and office managers don't make contract decisions the first time they hear from you.

Here's what actually moves the needle.

The cleaning companies winning recurring office contracts through cold outreach are running sequenced campaigns — typically 4 to 6 touchpoints over 3 to 4 weeks. Each message is short, relevant, and adds a small reason to reply. Not a pitch. A reason.

They're also sending to tightly defined lists. Not every business within 20 miles. Specific verticals — property management firms, multi-site office occupiers, healthcare admin buildings — where contract cleaning spend is predictable and renewals are regular.

But the part most cleaning businesses skip entirely is the follow-up structure after a reply comes in. Someone says 'we're up for renewal in three months' and the response is either an immediate sales call they're not ready for, or silence. Both kill the opportunity.

What works is a simple nurture sequence that keeps you visible, builds credibility, and books the conversation at the right moment — not when you want it, but when they're ready for it.

This is what a lead generation system looks like versus a lead generation attempt. The difference isn't budget. It's consistency, targeting, and follow-through.

If your pipeline is inconsistent — busy one month, dry the next — it's almost always because there's no outbound system running in the background.
