# Email Newsletter: Why FMs ignore your cold emails

**Subject Line:** Why FMs ignore your cold emails
**Preview Text:** It's not your service — it's how you're pitching it. Here's what actually works.
**Word Count:** 328

Hi [First Name],

Most cold emails to facilities managers get deleted in under three seconds. Not because the cleaning company isn't good — but because the email looks exactly like the last ten they received.

Facilities managers in offices, healthcare, and education are busy people with real accountability. They're not looking for the cheapest quote. They're looking for a contractor who won't create problems for them.

When your outreach is generic — "We offer professional cleaning services at competitive rates" — it signals that you haven't thought about their specific situation. It gets filed under ignore.

Here's what actually works: a 3-email sequence built around their world, not yours.

Email one references their sector. A multi-site office FM has different priorities to a school bursar or a clinic manager. Show you understand the difference — compliance requirements, footfall patterns, out-of-hours access. One or two specific lines is enough to stand out.

Email two addresses their building. Rough square footage, likely cleaning frequency, the kind of contract they're probably running now. You're not guessing wildly — you're demonstrating that you've done basic research and can think operationally.

Email three is a soft close. Not a pushy "last chance" — a simple invitation to compare what they're currently paying against what a scoped proposal from you would look like.

This sequence converts at 3 to 5 times the rate of a single generic pitch. The reason is simple: specificity builds credibility before you've even spoken to them.
