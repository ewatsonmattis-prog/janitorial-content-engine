# Email Newsletter: Why your cold emails get ignored

**Subject Line:** Why your cold emails get ignored
**Preview Text:** It's not your price. It's not your service. Here's what's actually happening.
**Word Count:** 342

---

Hi [First Name],

Most cleaning companies sending cold emails are doing the same thing: a one-line intro, a list of services, and a 'let me know if you're interested.' Then silence.

The problem isn't that office managers don't need cleaning. They do. The problem is that your email looks like the last five they deleted without reading.

Here's what's actually going wrong.

Generic outreach treats every prospect the same. The same template goes to a 50-person accountancy firm and a 200-person logistics company. Neither feels like you're talking to them. Neither replies.

The companies winning recurring office contracts aren't sending more emails. They're sending better ones — targeted by building type, decision-maker role, and a specific reason to reach out now. That specificity builds enough trust to get a reply.

But a reply isn't a contract. That's where most cleaning companies drop the ball.

No follow-up system means warm leads go cold. A prospect who was genuinely interested last Tuesday gets distracted, and you never come back. A consistent follow-up sequence — three to five touchpoints over two to three weeks — keeps you visible without being annoying. That's how interest turns into a booked conversation.

The other thing that's missing is a clear next step. 'Let me know if you're interested' is not a CTA. 'Here's a 15-minute slot to see if we're a good fit' is. Make it easy for them to say yes.

If you're sending cold emails and getting nothing back, the answer isn't to stop. It's to build a system that targets the right contacts, follows up consistently, and earns the conversation.
