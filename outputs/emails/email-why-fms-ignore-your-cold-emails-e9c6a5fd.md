Hi [First Name],

Most facilities managers delete cleaning pitches before they finish the first line.

Not because they don't need a contractor. Because the email reads like it was written for no one in particular.

"We provide high-quality commercial cleaning services across a range of sectors." That sentence is in roughly 80% of cold outreach FMs receive. It tells them nothing about whether you understand their building, their schedule, or their compliance requirements.

Here's what actually works.

Facilities managers shortlist contractors based on two things: responsiveness and specificity. They want to know you've done your homework — that you understand whether they're running a 40,000 sq ft NHS-contracted healthcare facility or a multi-tenancy office block with weekend access restrictions.

A generic pitch signals that you'll be equally generic when something goes wrong on site.

A 3-email sequence built around specificity — referencing their sector, building type, likely cleaning frequency, and the compliance standards relevant to them — consistently converts at 3 to 5 times the rate of a standard pitch.

Email one: Show you understand their environment. Reference their sector and what that means for cleaning protocols. No pitch yet.

Email two: Introduce one specific outcome you've delivered for a similar site. Keep it to two sentences. Link to a short case study if you have one.

Email three: Make the ask. One clear, low-friction next step — a 15-minute call, a site visit, or a sample schedule.

That's it. Three emails. Sent over ten to fourteen days. Focused entirely on their world, not yours.

If your current outreach is getting low reply rates from offices, schools, or healthcare facilities, the problem almost certainly isn't your pricing or your service. It's that nothing in your email makes the FM feel like it was written for them.
