Hi [First Name],

Most cleaning companies chasing office contracts have the same problem — and it's not a lack of leads.

They send a batch of cold emails, get a handful of replies, follow up once or twice, and then move on. The pipeline looks busy. The calendar stays empty.

The real issue isn't volume. It's the absence of a system that builds enough trust to turn a cold prospect into a booked conversation.

Facilities managers and office decision-makers are not going to hire a new cleaning contractor after one email. They're managing multiple suppliers, fielding dozens of outreach messages, and have usually had a bad experience with a cleaning company at some point. Trust is low by default.

What actually works is a sequence that earns attention over time. A strong opening email that leads with their problem, not your service list. A follow-up that adds a specific reason to reply — a relevant case study, a local reference, a direct question. A third touch that creates soft urgency without being pushy.

Most cleaning companies send one email and call it done. The ones winning recurring contracts are the ones still following up on day 14 when their competitor has already given up.

Consistency also matters more than cleverness. A simple five-step cold email sequence, sent to the right contacts in the right sectors, outperforms a single well-written email every time. The message doesn't need to be perfect. The system needs to keep showing up.

Once a prospect replies — even to say "not right now" — that's the moment most cleaning companies lose the deal. A proper CRM workflow, a scheduled callback, a calendar reminder three months out. That's how interest becomes a contract six months later.

If your current outreach relies on hoping someone replies before you forget to follow up, that's not a lead generation problem. That's a system problem.
