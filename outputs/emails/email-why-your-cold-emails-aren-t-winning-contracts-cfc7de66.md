**Subject Line:** Why your cold emails aren't winning contracts
**Preview Text:** It's not a leads problem. It's a system problem — here's the difference.

Hi [First Name],

Most commercial cleaning companies chasing office contracts have the same frustration: they send emails, maybe make a few calls, and then hear nothing back.

So they buy more leads. Or try a different list. Or give up on outreach entirely.

The problem isn't the leads. It's the absence of a system behind them.

Here's what actually happens when a facilities manager or office contact receives a cold email from a cleaning company: they might be mildly interested, but they're not ready to switch suppliers today. They file it away mentally, or delete it, and move on. Without a follow-up sequence, that's the end of the conversation.

Research consistently shows that most B2B decisions require between five and eight touchpoints before a meeting is booked. Most cleaning companies stop at one or two. The companies winning contracts aren't necessarily sending better cold emails — they're following up when their competitors have already gone quiet.

This is where a cold email system changes the outcome. Not a one-off blast, but a structured sequence: an opening email that speaks directly to the prospect's situation, followed by a short follow-up that adds value, then a soft check-in that keeps the door open. Each message builds familiarity without pressure.

The goal isn't to hard-sell a cleaning contract on the first email. The goal is to earn enough attention to get a conversation. From there, your sales process takes over.

There's one more piece that most cleaning companies miss: consistency. A system only works if it runs every week, not just when someone has spare time. The cleaning companies generating a predictable flow of new contract opportunities have taken outreach out of the to-do list and made it a repeatable process.

If your current approach to office contract lead generation is sporadic, or relies entirely on referrals and renewals, this is the gap worth closing first.

The good news is that a well-built cold email system doesn't require a big team or a large budget. It requires the right targeting, the right messaging, and the discipline to follow up.
