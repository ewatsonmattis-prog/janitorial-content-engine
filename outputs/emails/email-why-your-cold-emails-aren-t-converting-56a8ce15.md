Hi [First Name],

Most commercial cleaning companies we speak to aren't short on leads. They're short on a system that does anything useful with them.

You've probably sent cold emails before. Maybe you even got a few replies. But without a structured follow-up sequence, most of those conversations die before they ever reach a sales call. That's not a lead problem. That's a process problem.

Here's what tends to happen: a facilities manager opens your email, thinks "we might need this later", and moves on. No reply. Not because they weren't interested — but because you didn't give them a reason to respond right now, and you didn't follow up when the moment passed.

Effective cold outreach for office cleaning contracts isn't about sending more emails. It's about sending the right sequence — one that builds familiarity, demonstrates credibility, and makes it easy for the right prospect to raise their hand when the timing is right for them.

A system that works typically looks like this: a sharp first email focused on a specific pain point relevant to their building type or sector. A follow-up two to three days later that adds context or social proof. A third touch that creates a soft deadline or asks a direct question. Most cleaning companies stop at one email. The ones winning contracts are following up three to five times, across multiple weeks.

Trust is built through consistency, not volume. A facilities manager who sees your name in their inbox three times — with something relevant each time — is far more likely to book a call than one who received a single generic pitch six months ago.

The difference between cleaning companies that grow steadily and those stuck chasing one-off jobs is almost always the same thing: a repeatable outreach system that keeps their pipeline moving without relying on referrals or luck.

If your current cold email process stops after the first send, you're leaving contracts on the table every single week.
