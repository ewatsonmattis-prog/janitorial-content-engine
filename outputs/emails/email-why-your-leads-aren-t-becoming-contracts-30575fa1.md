Hi [First Name],

Most cleaning companies don't have a lead generation problem. They have a lead conversion problem.

You've probably sent cold emails, maybe tried a few LinkedIn messages, perhaps even paid for leads from a directory or broker. Some of those prospects replied. A few seemed interested. Then nothing happened.

That's not bad luck. That's a missing system.

Facilities managers and office procurement teams don't hand over a recurring contract to a company they've heard from once. Commercial cleaning is a trust-dependent sale. The company that wins the contract is rarely the first one to reach out — it's the one that stayed visible, followed up professionally, and gave the buyer a reason to take the next step.

Here's what a working cold email system for office cleaning contracts actually looks like:

First, it targets the right people. Not generic business databases — actual decision-makers at businesses with 20+ staff, property managers with multiple sites, or facilities leads at schools and healthcare sites. Specificity matters.

Second, it leads with relevance. A cold email that says "we provide competitive office cleaning" goes nowhere. One that references a specific pain point — inconsistent service from their current contractor, compliance concerns, high staff turnover from cleaning suppliers — gets a reply.

Third, it follows up without being annoying. Most replies come from the third, fourth, or fifth touchpoint. A single email is not a campaign. A structured sequence of five to seven messages, spaced correctly, with different angles, is a campaign.

Fourth, it has one job: book the call. Not close the contract. Not quote over email. Get one conversation booked with someone who has a cleaning requirement. Everything else happens from there.

If your current outreach doesn't have these four components running consistently, you're leaving recurring contracts on the table every single month.
